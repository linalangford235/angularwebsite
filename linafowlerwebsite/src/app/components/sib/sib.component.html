Self Image Beauty

Project Brief:

Self Image Beauty is a beauty company that offers esthetic services and lash training courses.

The client needed a simple and effective way to reach their target audience for training courses and beauty treatments.

My goal was to create a company website that encompasses the esthetic services as well as the training course information.

Note: to ensure I comply with my non-disclosure agreement I have omitted or changed names, designs, logos, etc. in this case study. All information in this case study is my own and does not represent the views of Self Image Beauty.

My Role

I worked with the client one on one to determine the needs of the product.

I did all the research, UX & UI design as well as all the code and deployment process and hosting for the application.

MY PROCESS

Research

Interview client to get a better understanding of the problem we are trying to solve.

Define

Create personas, role objectives, decisions, and understand client pain points.

Ideate

Create sketches, low fidelity wireframes, user flows, and gather client feedback.

Design

Create detailed designs, style guide, and gather client feedback.

Research & Define

Before I started sketching out any design, I sat down with the owners of Self Image Beauty to get a better understanding of the product I was to build.

After multiple interviews with the client I created a persona based on the user that would be visiting the site.

Ideation

In the ideation phase I sketched out ideas, created wireframes and came up with the basic idea for the web application.

I took these ideas back to the client to gather feedback and ensure we were on the same page.

Design

After completing the wireframes and gathering feedback from the client I started on the high-fidelity mocks.

After approval from the client I proceeded to built the application out in code and set up hosting for the client.

Reflection

I learned a lot through this design process. Mainly it's crucial to research your target audience before beginning the design process.

I also learned the benefits of having a prototype ready and available for the client to check through before beginning development. This allows for less changes after development has started.
