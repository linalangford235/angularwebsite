Deployment Dashboard

Project Brief:

HealthEquity is an HSA Administrator with a web portal where members and employers can access their financial information for their HSAs.

The problem the department faced was Scrum teams did not have an adequate way to validate the release of our monolith enterprise application to ensure teams did not have failing tests or bugs that would effect other parts of the application before being deployed to production.

Our goal was to create a web application that brought in all the data for each release cycle to one place where the SCRUM teams could check if a team is ready to release or not.

My Role

I led the design process for the Deployment Dashboard from strategy to production and presented all deliverables to stakeholders.

I also led the front-end development for the product and wrote the code to build out the Dashboard UI and front-end functionality while the backend was created by the other two devs on my team.

MY PROCESS

Research

Interview users to get a better understanding of the problem we are trying to solve.

Define

Create personas, role objectives, decisions, and understand user pain points.

Ideate

Create sketches, low fidelity wireframes, user flows, and gather user feedback.

Design

Create detailed designs, style guide, stakeholder reviews, and gather user feedback.

Test

Live prototype is created and begin user testing. Gather user feedback.

Research & Define

As part of the research step in my process, I conducted interviews with our users to understand pain points and issues we were trying to solve.

With the research I collected about our users I built personas to make sure we were keeping our users at the center of our product.

Ideation

After doing the research it was clear we needed a dashboard of some kind that all our users had access to view other teams deployment statuses.

This led me to start sketching out ideas and start designing the low-fidelity wireframes to get an idea on potential solutions to display the data we needed.

Design

After gathering more feedback from the users on the wireframes I begun the design process for our high fidelity mockups.

I created prototypes to test with our users to ensure we were gathering constant feedback.

Testing

After the prototype was built, we tested the product with our users through one on one interviews and group based testing.

After testing had completed we gather feedback from our users on ease of use, overall rating, pain points, etc. and our users rated our application 4.6 out of 5 stars.

Reflection

Over the course of this project I learned many things. Firstly, research is so important. Taking the extra time to thoroughly research the problem we were trying to solve resulted in a better user experience overall.

Next, learning to consider feedback at every stage was very beneficial to the product design. I learned it's always best to keep the users at the center of the design.
